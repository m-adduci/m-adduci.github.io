Work & Research

WORK

I currently support the Worldwide Human Geography Data (WWHGD) Working Group in a geospatial analyst capacity within its Data Team.

The working group takes a unique approach to create a human geography data framework that can leverage ongoing efforts around the world to identify, capture, build, share, and disseminate the best available structured and unstructured foundation data to our client.

I also have several years of experience as a 3D terrain developer, crafting geo-specific terrains for use in simulations. This work involved much attention to detail, and an in-depth knowledge of client expectations in creating purpose-built terrain.

It is fair to say that I have been exposed to multiple industries as a contractor, which has made the varied implementation of GIS even more rewarding.

RESEARCH

My primary academic research focus at The University of Illinois was an article in Geopolitics, Volume 3 titled “Mapping the Dynamism of the United States' Geopolitical Code: The Geography of the State of the Union Speeches, 1988–2008”

The work focused primarily upon the changing geographical foci of the geopolitical code of the United States are examined by a content analysis of the presidential State of the Union Speeches between 1988 and 2008. The State of the Union speeches are interpreted as geopolitical discourse within a structural setting, using seven foreign-policy paradigms as an organizing framework.

The empirical findings illustrate an increase over time in the number of regions and countries mentioned in the speeches. Also, notable differences between administrations in terms of their advocacy of globalist or regionalist policies and emphasis upon allies or adversaries are found.
